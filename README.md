# show

Visible proof surface for `qvra`.

`show` turns curiosity into contact.
It exists to make outcomes visible quickly and route strangers toward deeper utility, experimentation, or comparison.

## What belongs here

Artifacts in `show` should provide one or more of:
- demos
- examples
- showcase apps
- visible proofs
- compact walkthroughs
- output-first surfaces that make the field easier to enter

## What does not belong here

`show` is not:
- screenshot storage
- marketing veneer
- static display with no route to the underlying artifact
- proof without a path

## Anchor standard

Anything in `show` should have:
- visible outcome
- fast explanation
- direct route to source or next action
- enough clarity that someone can decide quickly whether to click deeper

## Current role in qvra

`show` is one of the gravitational anchors.
It converts visual curiosity into stars, forks, deeper clicks, and field entry.

Related routes:
- [`run`](https://github.com/qvra/run) for immediate tools
- [`bench`](https://github.com/qvra/bench) for measured claims and constrained comparison
- [`lab`](https://github.com/qvra/lab) for unstable work behind some visible proofs
- [`pulse`](https://github.com/qvra/pulse) for the visible rhythm of what is moving now

## Display rule

A visible proof that does not route to real contact weakens the field.
